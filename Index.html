Strategy 1
Prepayment

The Extra EMI

💡 How it works: Once a year, you pay one extra EMI as a direct principal prepayment — on top of your regular EMIs. This reduces your outstanding balance faster, cutting both tenure and interest.

When to do it: Bonus month, tax refund, or any windfall. Even one extra EMI a year makes a huge difference over 20 years.

Strategy 2
Step-Up

The Annual Step-Up

💡 How it works: Every year, you increase your monthly EMI by a fixed percentage. As your salary grows, your EMI grows with it — channelling more money into principal repayment.

Why 7.5%? It mirrors the average annual salary increment in India. But if you get 10–12% hikes, you can set it higher. Even 5% makes a big impact.

Hybrid

The Hybrid Power

💡 How it works: Combines both strategies — you increase your EMI annually and make an extra lump-sum prepayment once a year. Double attack on your principal.

This is the most powerful. The tenure reduction here is dramatically better than either strategy alone. Best suited if you're disciplined about saving.
